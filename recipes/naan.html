Naan

Ingredients

  • 2 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 2 cups warm water
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 6 tablespoons milk
  • 2 large egg, beaten
  • 4 teaspoons salt
  • 9 cups bread flour
  • 4 teaspoons minced garlic (Optional)
  • ½ cup butter, melted

Directions

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water in a large bowl. Let stand about 10 minutes, until frothy.
  2. Stir sugar, milk, egg, and salt into the yeast mixture. Mix in enough flour to make a soft dough.
  3. Knead dough on a lightly floured surface until smooth, 6 to 8 minutes.
  4. Place dough in the prepared oil, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  5. Punch down dough on a lightly floured surface, and knead in garlic. Pinch off small handfuls of dough about the size of a golf ball; you should have about 14. Roll each piece into a ball and place on a tray. Cover with a towel, and allow to rise until doubled in size, about 30 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, preheat a large grill pan over high heat.
  7. Roll each piece of dough into a thin circle.
  8. Brush some melted butter on the preheated grill pan. Place a few pieces of dough in the pan (as many as you can fit) and cook until puffy and lightly browned, 2 to 3 minutes. Brush butter onto the uncooked sides, flip, and cook until browned, 2 to 4 more minutes. Remove from the grill and repeat to cook the remaining naan.
  9. Enjoy!
